How Georgetown's Hard Water Damages Your Pipes

Most of the city's supply runs from Lake Georgetown, Lake Stillhouse Hollow, and Lake Belton through a Brazos River Authority contract, blended with groundwater drawn from the Edwards Aquifer. Both sources filter through limestone, which loads the water with calcium and magnesium. The result is roughly 25.8 grains per gallon, very-hard water that scales fixtures and wears copper pipe thin from the inside. Knowing where that water comes from is half of knowing where it tends to fail.

Georgetown's two big ones

Most Common Plumbing Leaks in Georgetown, TX

Two problems come up here more than anywhere else, and both trace straight back to Georgetown's water and its lawns.

Pinhole Leaks in Georgetown's Hard-Water Copper Pipes

Very-hard water does quiet damage. Every gallon that moves through a copper supply line leaves a little mineral behind, and over the years that scale pits the metal until a pinhole leak opens. You rarely see it coming. The first sign is often a spot of moisture on a wall, a faint drop in pressure, or a bill that creeps up for no reason. Sun City opened in 1995, and thousands of those homes still carry their original copper, which puts them right at the age when pinhole failures start. The mid-century pockets in Indian Creek and Heritage Oaks are even further along. We locate the failing run with acoustic gear, then repair or reroute just that section rather than guessing at the whole wall.

Sprinkler & Irrigation Leaks Under Georgetown's Watering Limits

Georgetown's master-planned neighborhoods run a lot of irrigation, and a cracked line or stuck valve underground can waste hundreds of gallons before anyone notices. During the city's drought restrictions, that hurts twice: once on the water bill, and again on a lawn that browns out while the leak drinks your allotment. The tell is usually a patch that stays green and soggy while everything around it goes dry, or a zone that never seems to build pressure. We pressure-test the irrigation system and pinpoint the break without trenching the whole yard, so the repair is a small dig instead of a torn-up lawn.

Georgetown Neighborhoods & Service Areas

The age of your street tells us a lot about what is likely failing and where to look first.

Old Town and the homes around the historic courthouse square date back generations, with galvanized supply lines and cast-iron drains that show their age. We adjust the approach for each, because a 1990s Sun City slab and a 2020s Wolf Ranch slab rarely fail the same way. Common questions

Georgetown Leak Detection — Frequently Asked Questions

How do you find a leak without tearing up my house?
We start with the least invasive tools we have. Acoustic sensors listen for the sound of water escaping a pressurized line, thermal cameras read the temperature difference a leak leaves behind, and pressure or tracer-gas testing confirms the spot. By the time anyone opens a wall or breaks concrete, we already know the location within inches.
My water bill doubled but I cannot see anything. What now?
A hidden leak is the usual culprit, often under a slab or behind a wall. Run the 60-second meter test first: shut off every tap and appliance, then watch the meter dial. If it keeps moving, water is escaping somewhere. Call us with what you find and we will help you read it.
Why does Georgetown water cause so many pinhole leaks?
Georgetown sits over limestone, and its water runs about 25.8 grains per gallon, very hard by any measure. That mineral load scales the inside of copper pipe and slowly eats through it, which is how pinhole leaks form. Homes from the 1990s, including much of Sun City, are now old enough for that wear to show.
Do you work in Sun City and the master-planned communities?
Yes. We cover Sun City, Wolf Ranch, Berry Creek, Cimarron Hills, Crystal Falls, Rancho Sienna, and the rest of Georgetown, plus nearby Round Rock, Cedar Park, Leander, Hutto, Liberty Hill, and Pflugerville. We treat each neighborhood on its own terms, since a 1990s slab and a newer PEX build rarely fail the same way.

Yes. Leaks do not keep business hours, and neither do we. Our line is staffed around the clock, so you reach a real person who can talk through the emergency and get help moving instead of an answering machine.
