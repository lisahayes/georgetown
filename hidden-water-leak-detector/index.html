Common questions about the meter leak test

How do I read my City of Georgetown water meter?
Open the meter box near your curb. The dial shows total gallons or cubic feet used. Note the number, wait 2 hours with no water use, then note it again. A difference means water is leaving the system somewhere between the meter and your fixtures.
Does a leak raise my Georgetown sewer bill?
No. Georgetown residential sewer is a flat monthly charge of $60.85. It does not change with water usage. A hidden leak raises your water bill only, never your sewer bill.
Why is my Georgetown water bill high with no visible leak?
Georgetown uses a tiered rate structure. A leak that pushes monthly usage from Tier 1 ($2.80 per 1,000 gallons) into Tier 2 ($4.20) or Tier 3 ($7.35) significantly raises your cost even if the physical leak seems small. A hidden slab leak, slow toilet flapper, or irrigation break are the most common causes.
Can a slab leak cause a high water bill in Georgetown?
Yes. A slab leak runs continuously because the supply system stays pressurized. Even a moderate slab leak can waste hundreds of gallons per day, pushing usage well into Tier 2 or Tier 3 on Georgetown water rates.
