Slab leak questions for Georgetown homeowners

What are the signs of a slab leak in Georgetown, TX?
The main signs are: unexplained spike in water bill, sound of running water when everything is off, warm or hot spots on the floor, a water meter that keeps moving with all fixtures closed, damp or buckling flooring, low pressure throughout the home, new wall cracks or sticking doors, and a wet or green patch of yard near the foundation.
Can Georgetown clay soil cause slab leaks?
Yes. Many Georgetown neighborhoods including Wolf Ranch, Rancho Sienna, Berry Creek, Crystal Falls, and Parkside on the River sit on expansive clay soil that swells when wet and shrinks in drought. That seasonal movement stresses pipes embedded under the slab, accelerating failures over time.
How do plumbers find a slab leak without breaking concrete?
Acoustic listening devices, electronic correlators, and thermal imaging cameras locate the leak through the slab. The concrete is only opened at the exact point of the repair, not for exploration.
Does a slab leak raise the water bill in Georgetown?
Yes. A slab leak runs continuously because the supply system is always pressurized. Even a moderate slab leak can push monthly usage from Tier 1 ($2.80 per 1,000 gallons) into Tier 2 ($4.20) or Tier 3 ($7.35), adding significantly to the monthly bill.
