Travis County, and we serve it

Pflugerville is an established suburb in Travis County, immediately south of Round Rock and northeast of Austin, which puts it just outside Williamson County. That does not put it outside our service area. We travel from Georgetown to Pflugerville regularly, because the practical distance is short and the plumbing challenges are familiar: established 1990s and 2000s homes carrying copper under the same hard water that affects the whole region, now reaching the age where leaks begin.

We say Travis County plainly because some homeowners are surprised. The county line is nearby, but it does not change how we work or what we charge.

1990s and 2000s homes in the aging window

Pflugerville's core was largely built through the 1990s and into the 2000s, making it similar in age to Georgetown's own established neighborhoods. Its original copper supply, installed when copper was the standard, has now spent 20 to 30 years under hard Hill Country water, corroding from within at the pace that water drives. That puts much of Pflugerville's housing in the window where pinhole leaks appear, and where one pinhole is rarely the last.

The story is the same one we know from Georgetown's own mid-tier neighborhoods: the copper has aged to the point where it deserves checking whenever a symptom appears.

Slab leaks and aging laterals

As the 1990s copper under Pflugerville's slabs ages, slab leaks grow more likely, showing as a warm spot on the floor or an unexplained drop in pressure. And the mature trees along established Pflugerville streets send roots into aging sewer laterals, causing slow drains and backups that a camera inspection diagnoses directly. Both are calls we handle regularly.

A Pflugerville home that has not had a plumbing inspection in years may have several of these developing quietly at once, and an honest condition check often finds what a single repair visit would miss.

Residential and commercial, both served

Pflugerville's commercial corridors along 130 and 45 see hard-use plumbing in retail centers, restaurants, and office parks that share the aging-copper issue at higher volumes. A slab leak under a commercial floor, a supply failure above a drop ceiling, or a spiking bill in a commercial building are calls we take here as well, handling them with the same non-invasive locate-first approach that keeps the work area small.

Because commercial plumbing moves more water, the hard-water scale and the corrosion pressure progress faster, which makes early detection more valuable in a commercial Pflugerville building than in a residence.

How we serve Pflugerville

We make the drive from Georgetown to Pflugerville for residential and commercial leak detection and repair, bringing acoustic, thermal, moisture, and pressure testing. We locate the leak before opening or digging, match the repair to the material and era, and test the system before leaving. Same-day and around-the-clock response is available for urgent leaks.

If a Pflugerville property shows a damp wall, a warm floor, fading pressure, slow drains, a sewer smell, or a spiking bill, we will make the trip and find the source. Travis County or Williamson County, the plumbing we find in Pflugerville is the same aging copper and hard water we deal with every day in Georgetown, and we come prepared.
