The community where pipe age tells the story

Sun City Texas, the Del Webb active-adult community on Georgetown's north side, opened in 1995 and has grown into thousands of single-story homes spread across three golf courses. For leak work, one fact matters more than any other here: those original homes were plumbed with copper supply lines that have now spent close to three decades carrying very-hard water, which puts much of Sun City squarely in the window where pinhole leaks begin.

That is why a damp patch on a wall, a slow drop in pressure, or a faint blue-green stain gets our attention quickly in Sun City. The neighborhood is reaching the age where one pinhole is rarely the last.

Single-story slabs and accessibility fixtures

Sun City homes are built for active-adult living: nearly all single-story, on concrete slabs, with accessibility-minded fixtures that show up in our work. Walk-in and low-threshold showers put extra demand on the waterproof pan beneath the tile, comfort-height toilets and single-lever faucets have their own seals, and the generous lots carry large irrigation systems that run hard through the Texas summer.

We handle all of it with the same care, from a slab line under the foundation to a shower pan that has started letting water out the base. None of these homes gets a copy-paste checklist.

Hard water, big lawns, and watering limits

Two local conditions drive a lot of Sun City calls. The first is the water itself, which runs about 25.8 grains per gallon and steadily corrodes the original copper from the inside. The second is irrigation. Sun City's lawns are extensive, and during Georgetown's drought restrictions a hidden sprinkler leak wastes the water allowance underground while the visible lawn browns out, costing twice over.

Between aging copper and heavy irrigation, pinhole and sprinkler leaks lead the list in Sun City, with slab leaks and scaled water heaters close behind.

How we work in Sun City

Whatever the leak, we locate it before opening anything, using acoustic, thermal, and pressure testing so a repair in your home stays small. We talk through what we found in plain language and let you decide on the fix, and we answer the phone around the clock, because a leak does not wait for office hours. For homes with multiple pinholes across original copper, we will give an honest read on whether a repipe makes more sense than another patch.

If your Sun City home is showing damp spots, fading pressure, or a soggy lawn, those are the signs its plumbing has reached the age worth checking.

A community we know street by street

Sun City spreads across distinct neighborhoods and three golf courses, Cowan Creek, Legacy Hills, and White Wing, built out in phases over the years. Those phases matter, because a home from the original mid-1990s sections has older copper than one built in a later phase, and that difference changes how likely a pinhole leak is and where we look first.

Knowing the community at that level lets us arrive with a sense of what a given street's homes tend to face, rather than starting from scratch. It is the kind of local read that turns a long search into a quick, confident one.

Questions

Leak detection in Sun City Georgetown

Why do Sun City homes get so many pinhole leaks?
Sun City opened in 1995, so much of its original copper supply pipe is now 25 to 30 years old, and Georgetown's very-hard water has been corroding it from the inside the whole time. That combination puts these homes right in the window where pinhole leaks start.
Do you work on walk-in showers and accessibility fixtures?
Yes. Sun City's walk-in and low-threshold showers, comfort-height toilets, and single-lever fixtures are common in our work here. We test shower pans carefully, since curbless designs put extra demand on the waterproof base.
Can you find a sprinkler leak without tearing up my lawn?
Yes. We pressure-test the irrigation zones and use acoustic locating to pinpoint a buried break, so the repair is a small targeted dig rather than a torn-up Sun City lawn that the HOA expects to stay neat.
