Larger lots by the river park

The neighborhoods around Garey Park, Georgetown's large park along the South San Gabriel River, tend toward bigger, greener lots than the city's denser subdivisions. That setting puts the emphasis on outdoor plumbing. Extensive irrigation keeps those larger landscapes green, and the water service lines run longer distances across the lots, which means the leaks we are called for most here are buried ones, irrigation and supply lines, rather than indoor pipe failures.

On a bigger lot near the park, a leak can sit far from the house, out where only the bill and a patch of soggy ground hint that anything is wrong.

Irrigation and buried lines lead the list

A larger lot near Garey Park often runs an irrigation system with many zones, and a single buried lateral or a valve that will not seal wastes water across a wide landscape, doubly costly under Georgetown's watering limits. The longer water service runs carry their own risk, a break far from the house that shows as fading pressure and a climbing bill long before any water surfaces.

Telling an irrigation leak from a supply-line leak is the first task in a yard that has both, and we sort that out before anyone digs.

Locating across a big lot

We do not guess at the wettest spot. We pressure-test the irrigation zones to find the one losing water, and for the buried supply line we trace its path and use acoustic correlation, sensors at two points calculating the leak's position between them, to pinpoint a break even far from the house. That turns a vague wet patch into a marked spot.

The payoff on a larger lot is a single targeted dig instead of a long trench chasing a line across green acreage near the park.

Repairs that fit the property

Once the break is located, the repair is one access at that spot. Where a long run or an irrigation line has aged out, replacement through the existing path keeps the surface damage small, which matters across a landscaped lot. We confirm the system holds and the ground stops taking on water before closing up, so a big lot does not hand you a repeat call.

Matching the method to the property, precise locating over dig-and-guess, is what keeps an outdoor repair near the park from turning the yard upside down.

How we work near Garey Park

We treat these larger lots for the outdoor leaks they hide, irrigation and buried supply lines, locating precisely before any digging, while still handling the indoor leaks any home can have. The mix of big lots and heavy irrigation along the river is specific to this part of Georgetown, and we approach it that way.

If a home near Garey Park has a soggy patch out in the yard, a climbing bill with a dry house, or fading pressure, the buried lines across the lot are the place to look. On a larger lot, the cost of waiting is measured in months of wasted water, so an early look pays for itself well before the next billing surprise.

Why are leaks near Garey Park mostly outdoors?
The larger, greener lots there run extensive irrigation and longer water service lines, so the leaks we are called for most are buried, irrigation and supply lines, rather than the indoor pipe failures common in denser neighborhoods.
Can you find a sprinkler leak without digging up my lot?
Yes. We pressure-test the irrigation zones to find the one losing water, then locate the break to a spot. The repair is a small targeted dig rather than trenching the irrigation across a large lot near the park.
My bill is up but the house is dry. Where is the leak?
On a big lot, that usually means a break out on the long supply or irrigation line, far from the house. We trace and correlate the buried run to find where it is losing water underground.
