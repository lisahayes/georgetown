What the 78628 ZIP covers

The 78628 postal area takes in west Georgetown, the side that has seen the most recent growth, running west of I-35 from the Wolf Ranch community toward the San Gabriel River and the newer subdivisions beyond. It gathers up much of Georgetown's newer-construction housing, including Wolf Ranch, Parkside on the River, Saddlecreek, and the larger-lot areas toward the western edge. An address in 78628 is most likely a newer home, and that changes the leaks we are called for.

We serve the whole ZIP, and in this part of Georgetown that mostly means modern plumbing with modern failure points rather than the corrosion of older areas.

Newer construction, newer materials

Homes across 78628 largely run PEX supply and PVC drains, materials that resist the hard-water corrosion that ages metal pipe. The leaks here are not pinholes in tired copper but failures at the connections: a fitting that was not fully made during the original build, a penetration where a line crosses the slab, or a drain joint that never fully set. A newer home is no guarantee against a leak, it simply moves the leak to a different place.

Knowing to look at the joints rather than the pipe is what finds a leak fast in this newer-growth side of the city.

Irrigation and appliances across the ZIP

The newer subdivisions in 78628 carry extensive irrigation across their lots, so a buried sprinkler break that wastes water under Georgetown's watering limits is a common call here, soggy patch and brown lawn included. And the appliance connections every home relies on, the dishwasher, the washing machine, the water heater, generate their share of leaks regardless of how new the house is.

Even with PEX resisting corrosion, the very-hard water across this ZIP still scales water heaters and fouls fixture parts over time, so those wear out on schedule.

The western edge and longer runs

Toward the western edge of 78628, the lots grow larger and the water and irrigation lines run longer, which adds a different kind of leak to the ZIP: a break far out on a long run, showing as fading pressure and a climbing bill long before any water surfaces. On those bigger lots we trace and correlate the buried line to pinpoint a break that can sit hundreds of feet from the house.

So the ZIP spans both compact newer subdivisions and larger western lots, and the approach shifts with the property.

How we serve 78628

Across this ZIP we locate the leak before opening or digging, whether it is a fitting behind a wall, a slab penetration, a buried sprinkler line, or a break out on a long western-edge run. We make the targeted repair and explain what failed, which in a newer home often points to whether other connections from the same build are worth a look.

If a property in 78628 has a damp spot, a soggy lawn, water at an appliance, or fading pressure on a large lot, we cover the whole ZIP and will find it. A newer home is no reason to wait and a larger lot is no reason to dig blind, and we treat both that way.
