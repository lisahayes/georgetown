What the 78633 ZIP covers

The 78633 postal area takes in northwest Georgetown, the side defined by its active-adult and golf communities. It is anchored by Sun City, the large Del Webb community, and includes Cimarron Hills, Berry Creek, the Reserve at Berry Creek, and established areas like Heritage Oaks and Indian Creek. An address in 78633 most likely sits in a planned community built from the 1990s onward, often single-story and built for an active-adult lifestyle, and that shapes the leaks we see.

We serve the whole ZIP, and across it the leak picture is driven by two things: 1990s copper reaching pinhole age, and the big irrigated lots and pools that come with golf-community living.

1990s copper reaching its years

Much of 78633 was built from the mid-1990s on, Sun City opened in 1995, which means a great deal of its original copper supply is now 25 to 30 years old. Under Georgetown's very-hard water, that copper has been corroding from the inside the whole time, putting much of the ZIP squarely in the window where pinhole leaks begin. A damp wall, a soft patch, or fading pressure gets our attention quickly here.

Because the same water has worked on every run, one pinhole in a 78633 home is rarely the last, so we check the surrounding pipe when we find one.

Big lawns, pools, and irrigation

Golf-community living means generous, irrigated lots, and across 78633 a hidden sprinkler leak that wastes water under Georgetown's watering restrictions is one of the most common calls, draining the watering allowance underground while the lawn browns out above. The pools and spas that come with many of these homes add their own plumbing, where a buried line, a fitting, or a spillover seal can quietly lose water.

Sorting an irrigation leak from a pool leak from a main-line leak is the first task in a yard that has all three, and we settle a suspected pool leak with a bucket test before going further.

Single-story slabs and accessibility fixtures

The active-adult homes that fill much of 78633 are largely single-story, built on slabs, with accessibility-minded fixtures that show up in our work, walk-in showers that demand a sound waterproof pan, comfort-height toilets, and single-lever faucets. Slab leaks under these homes turn up as a warm floor or an unexplained pressure drop, and the hard water scales their water heaters on a predictable schedule.

Newer custom homes in Cimarron Hills and the Reserve add modern plumbing to the ZIP, where leaks favor connections and the pool over corroded pipe.

How we serve 78633

Across this ZIP we locate the leak before opening or digging, acoustic and thermal for a copper pinhole or a slab line, pressure testing for irrigation, and the bucket, dye, and pressure tests for pools. We work carefully with accessibility fixtures, keep outdoor repairs to a small footprint that respects a manicured lot, and give an honest read on repair versus repipe where aging copper has begun to recur.

If a property anywhere in 78633 shows a damp wall, a soggy lawn, a pool losing water, or fading pressure, we cover the whole ZIP and will find it.
