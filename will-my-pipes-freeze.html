Georgetown pipe freeze questions

At what temperature do pipes freeze in Texas homes?
Texas homes are not built with the pipe insulation that colder-climate homes rely on. Exposed pipes, pipes on exterior walls, and pipes in crawl spaces or garages can freeze at 28 to 32 degrees Fahrenheit, especially after 6 or more hours of sustained cold. Pipes in interior heated spaces can usually withstand temperatures down to the low 20s before risk becomes significant.
How long does it take for pipes to freeze in Georgetown, TX?
In Georgetown homes, uninsulated pipes in exterior walls or unheated crawl spaces can begin to freeze in as little as 3 to 6 hours at 20 to 27 degrees Fahrenheit. Below 20 degrees, the timeline shortens significantly. Central Texas construction does not include the insulation buffer that northern homes rely on.
How do I protect my pipes during a Georgetown freeze warning?
Let cold-water faucets drip at one drip per second, especially on exterior walls. Open cabinet doors under sinks. Keep heat at or above 55 degrees even if away. Disconnect and drain garden hoses. Cover outdoor hose bibs with foam insulating covers. Know where your main shutoff valve is before the freeze arrives.
Why did so many pipes burst in Texas in 2021?
Winter Storm Uri in February 2021 brought temperatures well below 20 degrees Fahrenheit for an extended period. Texas homes lack the pipe insulation and interior pipe placement that homes in colder states rely on. The sustained cold exceeded what unprotected Texas plumbing could handle, causing widespread failures across Williamson County and the entire state.
