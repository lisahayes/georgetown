Georgetown leak cost questions

How much does a dripping faucet cost per year in Georgetown, TX?
A steady drip at 60 drips per minute wastes about 3.15 gallons per day. At Georgetown Tier 1 rates that adds a few dollars a year, but if multiple faucets drip or usage is already near a tier boundary, the marginal cost per drip rises sharply at $4.20, $7.35, or more per 1,000 gallons.
Does Georgetown, Texas have tiered water pricing?
Yes. City of Georgetown residential water rates as of October 2025 start at $2.80 per 1,000 gallons (Tier 1, up to 7,000 gallons per month) and rise to $4.20 in Tier 2, $7.35 in Tier 3, $14.70 in Tier 4, and $18.40 in Tier 5. A base charge of $33.25 per month also applies.
Does a leak raise my Georgetown sewer bill?
No. Georgetown residential sewer is a flat monthly charge of $60.85. It does not change with water usage. A leak only raises the water portion of your bill, never the sewer charge.
How much water does a running toilet waste per month?
A running toilet typically wastes about 480 gallons per day, or roughly 14,600 gallons per month. At Georgetown water rates, that can push monthly usage into Tier 2 or Tier 3, adding $50 or more to your monthly water bill on top of the base charge.
